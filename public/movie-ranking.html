The Best Movies/Series according to Ujjawal

My top 3 movies/series of all-time.

Tamasha

This is my favourite movie.

  • It Speaks to the Struggle of Identity
  • Emotionally Honest and Raw
  • Unique Storytelling Structure
  • Iconic Music by A.R. Rahman
  • Ranbir Kapoor’s Performance

Broken But Beautiful

My all time favourite series

  • Realistic Portrayal of Love and Heartbreak
    • Unlike many romantic dramas, this series explores pain, vulnerability, and healing after loss or betrayal.
    • It captures the emotional messiness of unrequited love, breakups, and the long road to moving on.
  • Deeply Relatable Characters
    • Veer and Sameera (Season 1–2) and Agastya and Rumi (Season 3) feel real — flawed, broken, intense.
    • I connect because I often see myself in these characters' mistakes, passions, and emotional wounds.
  • Poetic and Touching Dialogues
    • The series is full of beautiful lines about love, pain, longing, and healing.
    • It’s almost lyrical at times, making it a favorite for those who appreciate emotionally rich and philosophical writing.
  • Soulful Music
  • Healing and Self-Love
    • The show isn’t just about love—it’s about letting go, healing, and learning to love yourself again.
    • It leaves viewers with a message: You can be broken and still find beauty in life again.

Bada Naam Karenge

Recently watched and loved it !

  • Nostalgic Rajshri Touch
    • Directed by Palash Vaswani and produced by Sooraj Barjatya's Rajshri Productions, the series brings back the warmth of classic family dramas like Hum Saath Saath Hain, offering a comforting escape from the intense narratives prevalent on OTT platforms today.
  • Relatable Modern Romance
    • The story follows Rishabh and Surbhi, a Gen-Z couple navigating love, ambition, and familial expectations. Their journey from a chance meeting during the COVID-19 lockdown to an arranged marriage setup adds layers of complexity and relatability.
  • Authentic Family Dynamics
    • The series delves into the intricacies of Indian family structures, portraying generational conflicts and the balance between tradition and modernity. It emphasizes mutual respect and understanding between elders and the younger generation.
  • Soulful Music and Visuals
    • Anurag Saikia's music, featuring tracks like "Cheater Balma" and "Tum Sab Jaane Kanha," complements the storytelling, while the cinematography captures the charm of small-town India, enriching the viewing experience.
